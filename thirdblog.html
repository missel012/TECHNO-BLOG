Mr. Karl Dominic Fajardo Guest Speaker
11th November, 2024

Revenue Model Insights: Social vs. Non-Social Enterprises

Revenue models are the cornerstone of every business. Mr. Karl Dominic Fajardo's lecture delved into the critical differences between social and non-social enterprises, along with hybrid approaches and the importance of diversifying revenue streams.

Social Enterprise Revenue Models

Social enterprises aim to address societal issues while generating revenue. Their models often focus on accessibility and affordability:

  • Donation-Based Model: Funding from individual or organizational donors.
  • Grants: Financial support tied to specific projects, typically from governments or NGOs.
  • Fee for Service: Revenue from activities such as workshops or training.
  • Membership Subscriptions: Recurring revenue from loyal supporters.
  • Social Licensing: Partnering with businesses to promote socially-conscious products or services.

Non-Social Enterprise Revenue Models

Non-social enterprises focus on profit generation with scalable revenue models:

  • Direct Sales: Selling products or services directly to customers.
  • Subscription: Offering monthly, quarterly, or yearly payment plans.
  • Freemium: Providing basic services for free while charging for premium features.
  • Ad Revenue: Generating income through advertisements.
  • Affiliate Marketing: Earning commissions by promoting other businesses' products.

Hybrid Revenue Models

Some businesses combine elements of both social and non-social revenue models, creating hybrid strategies tailored to their needs. This approach allows startups to maximize opportunities and adapt to market demands.

Key Takeaways

  • Startups should aim for 3-4 revenue streams to diversify and stabilize income.
  • Subscription models are particularly effective, contributing to 42% of the world's millionaires.
  • Monthly subscriptions are more practical in countries like the Philippines, where 78% of consumer marketing targets such plans.
  • Direct sales offer immediate cash flow but lack the stability of subscriptions.

Mr. Fajardo emphasized the importance of understanding consumer behavior, pricing strategies, and the evolving demands of the market to build a successful revenue model.
